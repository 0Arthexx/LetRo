Things To Do

Before traveling, do a little research on the places you will be visiting. Find out what kind of weather to expect. Is it damp? Is it hot? How about the height? Do some research about where you are going to live. You can check out vacation travel reviews and guides for the best travel tips in the area on our website.
The important thing is to remember to prepare yourself in advance to keep your health in shape so you can relax and enjoy your trip no matter what. With our tips for preparing for a trip abroad, all you have to do is pack your travel essentials, make a reservation, and get ready for the best travel experience.
Yes, this is technically legal advice, but being prepared will help you avoid unwanted situations while traveling. Stranded in a new city with no money and no identity is every traveler's worst nightmare. Make sure you bring your important documents, including your passport, driver's license, social security card, credit card, insurance card, and an extra set of clothes. Travelers' checks are also good to have in case your card is lost or stolen.

Beginner Guide

Beginner Guide

STEP 1

Invest in good equipment

If you are going to multiple places, a good backpack that fits you well and a good, comfortable pair of shoes will be your two most important investments before you leave home. If you’re going to splurge somewhere, splurge on these two essentials.

STEP 2

Purchase travel insurance

Look, no one wants to spend money on travel insurance for international travel. But do it anyway. Trust me on this one.

STEP 3

Plan your budget carefully

I highly recommend checking out my article on planning your travel budget, where I go into much greater detail, but a good rule of thumb is to plan your backpacking expenses as carefully as you can, then bring twice as much money as you’ll think you need.

STEP 4

Brush up on safety tips

First time traveller shouldn’t be afraid of exploring the world. But you should be smart about it while you’re traveling. Research safety tips for backpacking and traveling, and then stay aware of your surroundings and keep them front of mind during your trip.

STEP 5

Talk to your bank

Your bank will flag your account if you start spending money in a foreign country with no warning, and trust me, you don’t want to find yourself in a foreign country with no access to your bank account. You also need to talk to your bank and credit card companies about foreign transaction fees. If they’re really high, consider opening an account at a bank such as Charles Schwab, where they charge no foreign transaction fees and reimburse all ATM fees monthly.
